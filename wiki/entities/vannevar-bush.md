# Vannevar Bush

## Overview

Vannevar Bush was a scientist and engineer who served as Director of the Office of Scientific Research and Development during World War II (source: [[as-we-may-think-summary|As We May Think]]). He proposed the [[memex|Memex]] in 1945 — a vision for a personal, curated knowledge store with [[associative-trails|associative trails]] between documents (source: [[llm-wiki-concept|LLM Wiki]]). His vision is considered an intellectual predecessor to the LLM Wiki pattern and to hypertext systems more broadly (source: [[llm-wiki-concept|LLM Wiki]]).

## Key Details

- Proposed the [[memex|Memex]] concept in his 1945 essay "As We May Think" (source: [[llm-wiki-concept|LLM Wiki]])
- Envisioned a system where the connections between documents are as valuable as the documents themselves (source: [[llm-wiki-concept|LLM Wiki]])
- His vision was closer to a personal, actively curated knowledge base than to what the web eventually became (source: [[llm-wiki-concept|LLM Wiki]])
- The part he couldn't solve — who does the maintenance — is addressed by LLMs in the modern pattern (source: [[llm-wiki-concept|LLM Wiki]])
- His essay influenced Douglas Engelbart (interactive computing, the mouse), Ted Nelson (coined "hypertext"), and Tim Berners-Lee (the World Wide Web) (source: [[as-we-may-think-summary|As We May Think]])
- He argued that existing hierarchical classification systems were inadequate because the human mind works by association, not by alphabetical filing (source: [[as-we-may-think-summary|As We May Think]])
